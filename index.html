About Me

A clinical mindset, built through four years of real-world device work

I am a final-year Biomedical Engineering student at Rangsit University, graduating in 2026. My academic journey has centered on understanding how devices reach patients — not just how they are built — which is why I gravitated toward clinical research, hospital-based services, and competitions that required me to defend the value of a technology to non-technical audiences.

Beyond engineering, I am genuinely interested in the commercial and scientific side of MedTech: how clinical evidence is translated into product value, how healthcare systems make adoption decisions, and how companies like Johnson & Johnson bring solutions to patients at scale. These questions motivate my interest in roles such as Medical Affairs, Product Specialist, and Health Economics & Market Access.

I am looking for an internship environment where I can contribute meaningfully while learning from experienced professionals — and where scientific rigor and patient outcomes are taken seriously.

Projects

Clinical Problems I Chose to Work On

⚡ Device · Diabetes

Photobiomodulation for Blood Glucose Management

Unmet need: Non-invasive glucose management for T2DM patients with low medication adherence. Designed a 660 nm LED wearable vest with PWM control; conducted preliminary clinical protocol and submitted IRB proposal. Qualified for STL 2026 Demo Days — Central & Eastern region.

⚡ Device · Wound Care

Chronic Wound Care Control Device

Unmet need: Chronic wound complications disproportionately affect diabetic and elderly patients with limited access to advanced care. Built a therapeutic control device validated through competition judging by clinical and industry panels. Gold Medal I-New Gen 2024, Silver 2025.

🧠 Analytics · Mental Health

HRV-Based Stress Analysis System

Unmet need: Objective, low-cost stress monitoring for occupational and clinical settings. Developed a real-time HRV signal processing pipeline with ML classification — gaining understanding of how physiological biomarkers can support clinical decision-making.

🧠 Analytics · Cardiology

Cardiac Risk Prediction System

Unmet need: Early identification of at-risk patients before acute cardiac events. Built a predictive risk stratification platform — this project shaped my understanding of how data-driven tools fit into clinical pathways and where evidence gaps exist.

💻 System · Hospital Ops

Medical Equipment Management System (MEMS)

Context: Hospitals lack efficient digital systems for equipment tracking and accountability. Developed a PHP/MySQL web platform for borrowing workflows, inventory, and audit trails — deployed and used in real hospital settings as part of academic service work.

💻 System · Calibration

Medical Inventory & Calibration Management

Context: Accurate equipment records are critical for patient safety and regulatory compliance. Built a calibration inventory system with Masterlist management; took on the role of primary Masterlist officer during hospital deployment at Samkoke Hospital.
